# Proposal: Building a 1,000-Company Federer ICP Database for DeepThought

## 1. Objective

The objective of this project is to build a structured database of 1,000 Indian specialty manufacturing companies that match DeepThought's "Federer Company" Ideal Customer Profile (ICP).

The final output will help DeepThought identify high-quality founder-led or promoter-driven manufacturing companies that are technically differentiated, operating in attractive sectors, and likely to benefit from DeepThought's offerings.

This proposal outlines the research strategy, qualification framework, workflow, tools, quality controls, expected deliverables, and execution plan for scaling the research from a small sample assignment to a 1,000-company database.

---

## 2. Understanding of DeepThought's ICP

DeepThought's target companies are not ordinary manufacturing businesses. The focus is on companies that show signs of technical depth, operational seriousness, and long-term growth potential.

The ideal company is similar to a "Federer Company": focused, specialized, technically strong, and built with depth rather than hype.

### Key ICP Characteristics

1. **India-based manufacturer**
   - The company should have manufacturing operations in India.
   - Traders, dealers, distributors, and import-only businesses should be excluded.

2. **Specialty or technical manufacturing**
   - The company should manufacture differentiated products.
   - Examples include specialty chemicals, precision engineering products, industrial components, electronics, clean-tech components, medical devices, advanced materials, and similar technical categories.

3. **Promoter-driven or founder-led**
   - Preference should be given to companies where founders, promoters, or family owners are actively involved in business direction.
   - Leadership visibility is an important signal.

4. **Technical decision-maker presence**
   - The company should ideally have a founder, promoter, director, or senior leader with technical, engineering, chemical, manufacturing, or domain expertise.

5. **Growth-sector exposure**
   - The company should operate in sectors with demand tailwinds, such as exports, renewable energy, EV supply chains, agrochemicals, specialty chemicals, medical devices, semiconductors, electronics manufacturing, aerospace, defence, and industrial automation.

6. **Expansion or growth signals**
   - Signals may include hiring, new certifications, export presence, new product lines, capacity expansion, recent funding, new plants, active digital presence, or customer expansion.

## 4. Research Methodology

The research will follow a structured funnel approach.

### Stage 1: Company Discovery

The first step will be to build a broad discovery list of potential companies from multiple sources.

Sources will include:

- Google Search
- LinkedIn
- IndiaMART
- TradeIndia
- JustDial
- Tofler
- ZaubaCorp
- MCA data references
- Company websites
- Industry association directories
- Export directories
- Trade fair exhibitor lists
- Sector-specific buyer-seller platforms
- News articles and press releases
- Government industrial cluster lists

Example search queries:

- "specialty chemical manufacturers in Pune"
- "precision engineering companies in Coimbatore"
- "EV component manufacturers India"
- "pharmaceutical intermediate manufacturers Hyderabad"
- "industrial valve manufacturers Ahmedabad"
- "aerospace component manufacturers Bengaluru"
- "medical device manufacturers Chennai"
- "agrochemical intermediate manufacturers Gujarat"

### Stage 2: Initial Filtering

Companies will be filtered out if they are clearly:

- Traders
- Dealers
- Distributors
- Pure service providers
- Importers without manufacturing
- Very large public companies outside the intended ICP
- Companies with no clear technical manufacturing activity
- Companies with insufficient public information

### Stage 3: ICP Qualification

Each shortlisted company will be evaluated using the Federer ICP scoring framework.

| Criteria | What Will Be Checked |
|---|---|
| Manufacturer | Factory, plant, production facility, product catalog, certifications |
| India-based | Registered office, manufacturing unit, plant location |
| Differentiated | Technical products, R&D, patents, certifications, exports, process capability |
| Technical decision-maker | Founder or director background through LinkedIn, company profile, interviews |
| Growing sector | Sector demand, export relevance, EV/renewables/chemicals/medical/defence tailwinds |
| Growth signals | Hiring, expansion, new products, certifications, active website, press mentions |

### Stage 4: Scoring

Each company will receive a structured score.

Suggested scoring model:

| Score Area | Points |
|---|---:|
| Confirmed manufacturer | 20 |
| Technical differentiation | 20 |
| Promoter/founder-led signal | 15 |
| Technical leadership signal | 15 |
| Growth-sector exposure | 15 |
| Expansion/growth signal | 15 |
| **Total** | **100** |

Suggested classification:

| Score Range | Classification |
|---|---|
| 80-100 | Strong ICP fit |
| 65-79 | Good ICP fit |
| 50-64 | Possible ICP fit |
| Below 50 | Reject or deprioritize |

Only companies that meet the minimum qualification threshold should be included in the final 1,000-company database.

## 6. Quality Control Process

Since this project involves a large number of companies, quality control is critical.

### Verification Rules

1. Every included company should have at least two independent sources.
2. Manufacturing status should be verified through a product page, facility page, certification, company profile, directory listing, or official statement.
3. Founder or leadership information should be cross-checked where possible.
4. Claims from AI tools should never be accepted without manual verification.
5. Companies that appear to be only traders or distributors should be rejected.
6. Revenue or employee estimates should be marked as estimates unless confirmed by a reliable source.
7. Ambiguous companies should be tagged as "Review Needed" instead of being treated as qualified.

### Review Layers

The quality process can be divided into three layers:

| Layer | Purpose |
|---|---|
| Researcher-level check | Verify basic company information and sources |
| ICP-level check | Confirm the company fits the Federer criteria |
| Final audit | Review scoring consistency and remove weak entries |

## 7. AI Usage Plan

AI can improve speed, consistency, and organization, but it should be used carefully.

### Appropriate AI Use Cases

AI can be used for:

- Generating search queries
- Summarizing company websites
- Structuring company notes
- Identifying possible growth signals
- Creating sector-specific search strategies
- Drafting short company descriptions
- Standardizing scoring language

### Restricted AI Use Cases

AI should not be used as the final source of truth for:

- Revenue
- Founder identity
- Manufacturing confirmation
- Certifications
- Plant location
- Customer names
- Export claims
- Legal status

### Hallucination Control

To control hallucination risk:

- AI-generated company facts will be verified manually.
- Every factual claim will be linked to a source.
- Unsupported claims will be removed.
- Company inclusion will depend on evidence, not AI confidence.
- A "source confidence" note can be added for borderline cases.

## 8. Execution Plan

The 1,000-company database can be built in phases.

### Phase 1: Research Design and Template Setup

Activities:

- Finalize segments and geographies
- Create spreadsheet structure
- Define scoring rules
- Create rejection criteria
- Build source list

Output:

- Final research template
- Scoring guide
- Initial source directory

### Phase 2: Pilot Batch

Activities:

- Research 50-100 companies
- Test scoring consistency
- Identify unclear criteria
- Refine source strategy
- Review examples of strong, good, weak, and rejected companies

Output:

- Pilot company list
- Improved scoring rubric
- Quality-control checklist

### Phase 3: Scaled Research

Activities:

- Research companies by city and segment
- Maintain source links for every company
- Score each company
- Tag companies by priority level
- Reject unsuitable companies with reason codes

Output:

- 600-800 qualified companies
- Rejection log
- Segment-wise coverage tracker

### Phase 4: Final Completion and Audit

Activities:

- Complete 1,000 qualified companies
- Remove duplicates
- Normalize company names
- Check missing data fields
- Audit low-confidence entries
- Re-score inconsistent records

Output:

- Final 1,000-company database
- Summary dashboard
- Research methodology note
- Quality-control notes

## 11. Risk Areas and Mitigation

| Risk | Mitigation |
|---|---|
| Companies falsely claiming manufacturing | Verify through product pages, plant information, certifications, and multiple sources |
| Limited public data for MSMEs | Use directories, LinkedIn, MCA/Tofler references, trade fair listings, and website evidence |
| AI hallucination | Treat AI only as an assistant and manually verify every factual claim |
| Duplicate company records | Normalize names and check websites, CINs, locations, and promoter names |
| Inconsistent scoring | Use a fixed scoring rubric and conduct batch reviews |
| Overrepresentation of one segment | Track segment and city quotas during research |
| Weak founder data | Mark unavailable information clearly instead of inventing or assuming |

---

## 12. Success Metrics

The project will be considered successful if:

- 1,000 qualified companies are identified.
- Every included company has at least two source references.
- Manufacturing activity is reasonably verified.
- Each company has an ICP score and classification.
- Strong-fit companies are clearly separated from weaker-fit companies.
- The database is clean, deduplicated, and easy to filter.
- The final output helps DeepThought prioritize real outreach targets.

## 13. Conclusion

This 1,000-company research project will create a practical and scalable map of India's specialty manufacturing ecosystem for DeepThought.

The proposed approach balances speed with verification. It uses AI for research assistance and structure, but keeps final judgment grounded in manually checked evidence. By combining city-wise discovery, segment-wise targeting, source verification, and a clear scoring framework, the final database can become a high-quality outreach and market intelligence asset.

The project will not simply produce a large list of companies. It will produce a qualified, scored, and evidence-backed database of Indian specialty manufacturers that are most likely to match DeepThought's Federer ICP.
